Immediate Crisis Action Steps

  1. Get to a safe place - Remove yourself from immediate danger
  2. Call for help - Use the numbers above or call emergency services
  3. Stay with someone - Don't be alone if possible
  4. Remove harmful objects - Put away anything that could cause harm
  5. Use coping strategies - Try breathing exercises or grounding techniques
  6. Follow up with professional help - Make an appointment as soon as possible

Warning Signs to Watch For

Emotional Signs

  • Persistent sadness lasting more than 2 weeks
  • Feeling hopeless or worthless
  • Overwhelming anxiety or panic attacks
  • Extreme mood swings

Behavioral Signs

  • Thoughts of self-harm or suicide
  • Withdrawing from friends and activities
  • Significant changes in sleep or appetite
  • Substance use as coping mechanism

Physical Signs

  • Unexplained aches and pains
  • Extreme fatigue or insomnia
  • Difficulty concentrating
  • Changes in personal hygiene

Daily Wellness

Build mental resilience with these 5 evidence-based steps to wellbeing

1

Connect

Spend time with people you care about. Social connections are vital for mental health.

  • Have meaningful conversations face-to-face
  • Join groups or activities you enjoy
  • Reach out to friends and family regularly
  • Be present when you're with others
2

Be Active

Physical activity boosts mood and reduces stress. Find activities you enjoy.

  • Aim for 20-30 minutes of activity daily
  • Try walking, dancing, sports, or yoga
  • Start small and build up gradually
  • Exercise with friends for motivation
3

Keep Learning

Learning new skills builds confidence and provides a sense of accomplishment.

  • Try a new hobby or creative activity
  • Read books or listen to podcasts
  • Take online courses in subjects you're curious about
  • Learn from your mistakes and challenges
4

Give to Others

Helping others creates positive feelings and strengthens community connections.

  • Volunteer for causes you care about
  • Help friends, family, or neighbors
  • Practice random acts of kindness
  • Share your skills and knowledge
5

Take Notice

Practice mindfulness and be present in the moment. Notice the good things around you.

  • Practice gratitude daily
  • Spend time in nature
  • Use mindfulness and meditation techniques
  • Pay attention to your surroundings and feelings

üìö Stress Management by Category

Academic Stress

Time Management

  • Create daily schedules and use planners
  • Use Pomodoro technique (25 min work, 5 min break)
  • Break large tasks into smaller steps
  • Set realistic, achievable goals

Study Techniques

  • Take regular breaks during study sessions
  • Use active recall and spaced repetition
  • Create study groups for support
  • Find your optimal learning environment

Social Stress

Communication Skills

  • Use "I" statements to express feelings
  • Practice active listening
  • Be specific about your needs
  • Learn assertiveness techniques

Boundary Setting

  • Know your limits and communicate them
  • Be clear and direct when saying no
  • Offer alternatives when appropriate
  • Don't justify every decision

Technology Balance

Digital Detox

  • Set specific offline times daily
  • Use app timers to limit usage
  • Create phone-free zones at home
  • Find engaging offline hobbies

Mindful Social Media

  • Unfollow accounts that cause negativity
  • Limit comparison with others
  • Share positivity and kindness
  • Remember: social media shows highlight reels

Professional Help

Understanding when and how to seek professional mental health support

When to Seek Professional Help

It's time to reach out to a professional if you're experiencing:

Moderate

Persistent sadness or hopelessness

Lasting more than 2 weeks

Moderate

Anxiety interfering with daily activities

Ongoing difficulty functioning

High Priority

Thoughts of self-harm or suicide

Any occurrence requires immediate help

Moderate

Significant behavioral changes

Changes in sleep, appetite, social withdrawal

High Priority

Substance use as coping mechanism

Regular use to cope with feelings

Moderate

Difficulty functioning

Grades dropping, isolating from friends

Remember: Seeking help is a sign of strength, not weakness. Early intervention leads to better outcomes.
